Project Scope

Scope & Research Questions

The goal is a demonstrable, measurable, academically defensible prototype that directly answers three research questions from the systematic review.

RQ1

Aerodynamic drag & projectile energy loss

Custom long-step simulation loop, velocity decay via drag formula, air density as runtime variable, bullet drop via gravity integration per step.

UBallisticSimulatorAEnvironmentManager
RQ2

Bullet penetration through variable-density materials

Per-surface data asset, impact angle via dot product, energy threshold for penetration vs. stop vs. ricochet, exit velocity calculation.

UPenetrationResolverUMaterialPenetrationData
RQ3

Multiplayer sync & lag compensation for non-linear projectiles

Server-authoritative RPC, client prediction trace, delta logged to HUD, measured across 0/50/100/200ms Network Emulation tiers.

UWeaponComponentServerFire RPC
Scope boundary: One weapon type, one ammo type, a flat test level with material targets, two-player listen server. Not a playable game — a physics testbed.

SLR Findings Applied

SLR FindingImplementation Response
Hitscan lacks tactical depth and realismLong-step projectile simulation with full physics per step — no hitscan fallback
"Simulation-Latency Paradox" — complexity increases lag impactServer runs full sim; client receives only FBallisticImpact result (minimal payload)
Gap: dynamic weather integration for real-time projectile physicsAEnvironmentManager — runtime air density as replicated variable, switchable mid-session
Lag compensation needed for competitive fairnessClient prediction + server validation delta logged — measurable and chartable for thesis

Network Flow

Client: Player presses fire → RequestFire(AimDir) on UWeaponComponent
↓ ServerFire() RPC (Reliable, WithValidation)
Server: ServerFire_Implementation() → UBallisticSimulator::SimulateProjectile()
↓ reads AirDensity from AEnvironmentManager
Server: Long-step loop → hit → UPenetrationResolver::Resolve()
↓ FBallisticImpact produced
MulticastImpact(FBallisticImpact) → all clients receive result
↓ in parallel ↓
Client: Local prediction trace → predicted hit delta logged to HUD

Academic Foundation

Research Questions

From the Systematic Literature Review. Every implementation decision maps back to one of these three questions.

RQ1

How do current game engines model aerodynamic drag and projectile energy loss?

Addressed by UBallisticSimulator. Custom long-step integration using Fd = 0.5ρv²CdA replaces UE's default physics tick. Air density from AEnvironmentManager makes ρ a runtime variable — the gap identified in the SLR.

UBallisticSimulatorAEnvironmentManagerUAmmoDataPhase 2
RQ2

What are the most efficient algorithms for calculating bullet penetration through variable-density materials?

Addressed by UPenetrationResolver. Uses impact angle (dot product against surface normal), kinetic energy threshold, and per-material friction factor to determine penetration vs. stop vs. ricochet.

UPenetrationResolverUMaterialPenetrationDataPhase 3
RQ3

How do developers handle network synchronization and lag compensation for non-linear projectiles?

Addressed by UWeaponComponent. Server-authoritative via ServerFire RPC. Client runs a local prediction trace. Delta between prediction and server result logged to ABallisticDebugHUD and measured across 0/50/100/200ms tiers.
